Common Questions

What Investors Ask

What does it mean to be an accredited investor?+
An accredited investor generally meets one of the following: annual income exceeding $200,000 individually ($300,000 jointly) in each of the two most recent years, or net worth exceeding $1,000,000 excluding primary residence. Most physicians, surgeons, dentists, and high-income professionals qualify.
What is the minimum investment?+
Minimum investment amounts are covered in detail during the webinar and private calls, as they are specific to the deal structure and formal offering documents. Reserve your webinar seat to receive full investment details and data room access.
How is my capital protected?+
As a limited partner, your investment is backed by a tangible asset. Harmony Grove enters the deal with existing in-place income and strong occupancy (94.7%), providing operational stability from day one. Cobb County's fiscal conservatism further supports durable renter demand throughout the hold period.
What is the projected hold period?+
The targeted hold period is 3–7 years, allowing sufficient time to execute the value-add renovation program, stabilize the asset, and position it for an optimal sale or refinance event.
What are the tax advantages of this type of investment?+
Real estate syndications can offer significant tax benefits through depreciation deductions, which may offset or shelter a portion of your passive income distributions. We strongly encourage consulting your tax advisor. We are also happy to connect you with CPAs who specialize in working with physicians and real estate investors.
How involved do I need to be as a limited partner?+
Not at all — that is the core value of the limited partner structure. You receive regular investor updates, quarterly financial reporting, and distribution payments. There are no tenant issues, no maintenance calls, and no operational decisions required from you. You invest. The team manages. You collect.
Do the operators invest their own capital in the deal?+
Yes. Alignment of interests is a foundational principle at both Mila Penn Capital and Chazak Capital. The general partners invest their own capital alongside limited partners in every deal.
