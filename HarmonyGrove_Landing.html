Common Questions

What Investors Ask

What does it mean to be an accredited investor? +
An accredited investor generally meets one of the following criteria: annual income exceeding $200,000 individually (or $300,000 jointly with a spouse) in each of the two most recent years, or a net worth exceeding $1,000,000 excluding a primary residence. Most physicians, surgeons, dentists, and high-income professionals qualify. We are happy to discuss your specific situation during a private call.
What is the minimum investment? +
Minimum investment amounts are covered in detail during the webinar and private calls, as they are specific to the deal structure and formal offering documents. We encourage you to reserve your webinar seat or schedule a call to receive the full investment details and access the deal data room.
How is my capital protected? +
As a limited partner, your investment is backed by a tangible asset — real estate. Harmony Grove enters the deal with existing in-place income and strong occupancy (94.7%), providing operational stability from day one. The submarket's supply constraints, institutional employment anchors, and Cobb County's fiscal conservatism further support durable renter demand throughout the hold period. As with any investment, risks exist and are disclosed fully in the offering documents.
What is the projected hold period? +
The targeted hold period is 3–7 years, allowing sufficient time to execute the value-add renovation program, stabilize the asset, and position it for an optimal sale or refinance event. Full timeline details are discussed in the offering documents and during investor calls.
What are the tax advantages of this type of investment? +
Real estate syndications can offer significant tax benefits through depreciation deductions, which may offset or shelter a portion of your passive income distributions. Some investors — particularly those who qualify as real estate professionals under IRS guidelines — may be able to apply losses against active income. We strongly encourage consulting your tax advisor to understand how these benefits apply to your situation. We are also happy to connect you with CPAs who specialize in working with physicians and real estate investors.
How involved do I need to be as a limited partner? +
Not at all — that is the core value of the limited partner structure. You receive regular investor updates, quarterly financial reporting, and distribution payments. There are no tenant issues, no maintenance calls, and no operational decisions required from you. Your role is to review investor communications and attend optional updates as you choose. You invest. The team manages. You collect.
Do the operators invest their own capital in the deal? +
Yes. Alignment of interests is a foundational principle at Mila Penn Capital and Chazak Capital. The general partners invest their own capital alongside limited partners in every deal. This ensures that every operational decision, renovation investment, and exit strategy is made with the same sense of ownership that you bring as an investor.
